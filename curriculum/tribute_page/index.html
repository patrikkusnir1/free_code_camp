Albert Einstein

The genius who revolutionized physics with his theory of relativity.

Timeline of Albert Einstein’s Life:

  1. 1879Born in Ulm, Germany.
  2. 1884Received a compass from his father, sparking his fascination with science.
  3. 1895Failed his first entrance exam to the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich but later passed.
  4. 1900Graduated from the Swiss Federal Polytechnic with a diploma in mathematics and physics
  5. 1905Published four groundbreaking papers, including the Special Theory of Relativity (E=mc²), in his "Annus Mirabilis" (miracle year).
  6. 1909Became a professor of theoretical physics at the University of Zurich.
  7. 1915Completed the General Theory of Relativity, which redefined gravity.
  8. 1919Einstein’s theory was confirmed during a solar eclipse, making him world-famous.
  9. 1921Awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on the photoelectric effect.
  10. 1933Fled Nazi Germany and moved to the United States, joining the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton.
  11. 1939Signed a letter to President Franklin D. Roosevelt, warning about the potential of nuclear weapons (leading to the Manhattan Project).
  12. 1945Advocated for nuclear disarmament after World War II.
  13. 1952Declined an offer to become the President of Israel.
  14. 1955Died in Princeton, New Jersey, at the age of 76.
  15. 1960s – presentHis theories continue to shape modern physics, influencing fields like quantum mechanics and cosmology.
  16. 2015Gravitational waves, predicted by Einstein, were detected by LIGO, further confirming his work.
Einstein’s work paved the way for modern physics. His insights were not just revolutionary—they were a glimpse into the very fabric of reality.

Stephen Hawking
